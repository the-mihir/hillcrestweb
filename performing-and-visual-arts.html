JESSICA MENDOZA, SLC DIRECTOR

Director Ms. Mendoza

THEATRE TRACK OVERVIEW

Award-Winning Theatre Track

  • Learn production, directing, writing and performing as well as technical aspects of theatre including lighting, sound and set design.
  • Students of all grade levels have opportunities to perform in different settings: plays, musicals, open mic nights, the Winter and Spring Festival of the Arts and Thespian Show.
  • Partnership with the LeAp arts program providing an intensive playwriting residency that engages students in storytelling and creative expression.
  • APVA students have participated in competitions all over New York City including the August Wilson Monologues, National Shakespeare Competition, and LEAP’s playwriting competition.

Theatre track culminates to taking the Theatre Regents. Upon completion of 10 theatre credits and the Regents, students will earn a Chancellor's Arts Endorsed Diploma.

ART TRACK OVERVIEW

Comprehensive Art Track

  • Four-year art track for advanced students
  • Portfolio 1 and 2 surround students in a broad range of media including collage, printmaking, drawing, painting and sculpture
  • Portfolio 3 and 4 expands on students’ skills sets and knowledge with opportunities to work on large-scale projects using a broader array of materials
  • Graphic design and independent study offered during the 11th and 12th grades
  • Advanced Placement 2D Portfolio
  • Art Regents

Art track culminates to taking the Art Regents. Upon completion of 10 art credits and the Regents, students will earn a Chancellor's Arts Endorsed Diploma.

MUSIC TRACK OVERVIEW

Music Track

  • First year - Music History and Performance Level 1&2
  • Second Year - Beginning Band and Orchestra / Chorus
  • Third Year - Intermediate / Advanced Band or Orchestra / Chorus
  • Fourth Year - Intermediate / Advanced Band or Orchestra / Chorus

Music Electives:

  • Core Music 1&2 (Two Semesters)
  • Music Theory 1&2 (Two Semesters)
  • AP Music Theory 1&2 (Two Semesters)
  • Keyboard 1&2 (Two Semesters)

A LETTER TO APVA FAMILIES AND PROSPECTIVE FAMILIES

Dear APVA Families and Prospective Families,

This year the Academy of Performing and Visual Arts enters its third season as a comprehensive small learning community that offers a track in theater, visual arts and music. We are excited about the opportunities offered to our students through wonderful partnerships that provide exposure to the professional arts field.

For the 2023-2024 school year we’ve augmented our curriculum to include:

Musical Theater Programming: Our partnership with TADA will allow 9th, 11th, and 12th students to put on a musical under the guidance of professional choreographers and directors.

African Song and Drumming: We will be partnering up with Asase Yaa to embed this culturally enriching component to this year’s Arts Festivals.

Playwriting Seminar– our acting class will be enhanced with playwriting workshops that will culminate into the sophomore showcase.

As a small learning community, we value the arts for its potential to foster the social development of young adults and to provide life-long skills that will help them to succeed in any 21st century career.

Hillcrest is a STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics) school that proudly offers its students a world-class education with a dynamic faculty and challenging curriculum.
